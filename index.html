About TimeDilation

Perhaps you have heard of Time dilation and thought of time traveling, or the twin paradox where one twin travels to space and comes back younger. It gives you an idea of what time dilation is but does not really explain the theory itself. For this section, the theory of Time dilation will be explained and described in a less complicated way with an approach to give the reader an introduction to the subject.

Time dilation is described as how time passes differently for different observers based on their relative movement. Time is not absolute and the velocity, while time passes, is different for observers in different reference frames. Let us take an example of an event with time dilation: Imagine a mirror on the ground and a mirror on the roof of a train or fast-moving vehicle. Now imagine a light pulse traveling in between these two mirrors in the train. An observer inside the train will observe that the light pulse traveling up and down between the mirrors will move in a vertical motion. While a stationary observer watching the train will see the train shifted to the right and perceive that the light pulse gives an angled path.

This is known as ”Einstein’s Simple Clock” which was a thought experiment the scientist Albert Einstein came up with. He thought of what could have been the ”easiest clock” and in this case, each reflection of the light pulse is a tic on the clock. This is an example of a phenomenon with time dilation. Another example of time dilation is when two identical twins are exposed to different velocities. There is one stationary twin and another twin traveling near the speed of light. When the traveling twin comes back it appears to be a bit younger.

One thing to know is that we are all under the effect of time dilation but at a low level. The effect is much clearer when near the speed of light. When you are near that velocity is when you will experience that time runs much slower than if you were stationary. Experimenting with the theory of time dilation has been done many times before in different ways such as the ”Simple Clock” and also the ”Hafele Keating Experiment”. The space organization NASA has also tested the theory of time dilation. In 2019 NASA published a twins study result. There were two twins, Scott and Mark Kelly, Scott spent one year in space and Mark stayed on Earth. When Scott came back, it resulted through time dilation that the stationary twin on earth was 5 mili seconds younger. Because Scott did not travel any near the speed of light it resulted in his aging was not as apparent as you would see in the Sci-fi movie Interstellar. We have not achieved a velocity near the speed of light yet. If you were thinking of becoming younger than your classmates, going to space will not really solve that.

There are also formulas to count the time dilation between observers. This however can be seen in the Calculator section. Time dilation is in the theory of relativity which is a theory discovered by the scientist Albert Einstein. A quick explanation of the general and special theory of relativity will be found in that section of the website.

About the Theory of Relativily

The German scientist Albert Einstein invented the theory of relativity which consists of two parts, the special theory of relativity from 1905 and the general one from 1915. Einstein showed how time and space were interwoven that had not previously been realized.

General Relativity:

You can perceive the theory of relativity as a theory about gravity. Instead of seeing gravity as an invisible force that attracts objects to each other, you can perceive it as a distortion or curvature of space. The larger the object is, the more space will distort around it. Imagine yourself a heavy ball that lands on a piece of paper and then the sheet becomes distorted. Instead of mass being drawn to mass, the ball follows the curvature of the room.Gravity can also distort time. Let’s take another example. Imagine a person climbing to the top of a mountain with his clock, now his clock would start to tick a lot faster than a person who would be at the bottom of the mountain. This is due to the difference in gravity in each location.

In the general theory of relativity, it is shown that gravity curves space and time. There have been multiple experiments done to prove the general theory of relativity. One example was a star named Stein 2051 B that seemed to change position in the sky but in reality, the position had not changed. The effect of ”gravitational lensing” occurred due to the curvature of space.

Special Relativity:

In the theory of special relativity, Einstein explained how time and space were connected to each other. There are two basic concepts to know about

  • As long as an object continues in a straight motion at a constant speed (without acceleration), the laws of physics apply the same to everyone.

  • *The speed of light travels at an unchanging speed of 186 000 km per second.


    • Important notice is that no matter how fast an observer moves or how fast an object emits light rays, measuring the speed of flight will always give the same results. Let us give you an example of an effect that can occur with special relativity – Picture yourself in a high-speed aircraft and you suddenly pass your friend who is in an identical craft but at a much slower speed. You will start to see that time goes much faster for you (Read Timedilation). If your aircraft increases its speed, both your mass and the mass of the craft will increase. The faster you go, the heavier you and your aircraft will be and the more will your aircraft withstand your efforts to go faster. Another thing that will also occur when you travel that fast is that your friend's aircraft will seem smaller than yours although they are identical. With these points, Einstein came to the conclusion that nothing with mass can achieve the speed of light.

    You might have also heard about the infamous equation: E = mckvadrat (E stands for energy, m for mass, c for the speed of light). The equation is a consequence of special relativity and also shows that energy and matter are interchangeable with the help of the equation.

    Time Dilation Equation:

    Δt' = Δt0 / √(1 - v²/c²)

    t0 is the time that has passed for a observer in movement.
    (Time dilation can also be counted with the time of an observer
    that is stationary, but has not been implemented on the website).

    v is the speed for the travelling observer (m/s)

    c is the speed of light (299 792 458 m/s)

    Lorentz Factor:

    γ = 1√(1 - v²/c²) = γ * Δt0

    γ is known as ”The Lorentz Factor” and is used as a factor within the Time dilation equation. The lorentz factor is included in Lorentz Transformation which is a set of equations.

    If Δt0 or Δt is not a given number for a problem equation, it can be calculated through other equations that can be seen in College text books like University Physics Volume 3.
